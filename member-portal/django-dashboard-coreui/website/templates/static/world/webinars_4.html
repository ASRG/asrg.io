"Lessons Learned for Automotive Navigation after the Tesla GPS Hack"

  • Join the LiveStream

    • Next LiveStream

    When: May 6th, 2020 @ 12:00 EST (Detroit), 18:00 CEST (Berlin), 19:00 Tel-Aviv and Romania

    Where: LiveStream

    Who:
    Roi Mit (CMO, Regulus Cyber)
    Dror Katalan (Chief Engineer, Regulus Cyber)

    Presentation:

    Regulus is one of the software leaders for GPS security. GPS is widely used across multiple automotive applications and in extensive use across multiple vehicles on the road today. The Regulus Cyber researchers found that spoofing attacks on the Tesla GNSS (GPS) receiver could be carried out wirelessly and remotely, exploiting security vulnerabilities in mission-critical telematics, sensor fusion, and navigation capabilities. This talk will share the current state of GPS technology in vehicles, present existing navigation systems in both ADAS and autonomous vehicles, and detail the GPS spoofing experiment and the results. Regulus' experts will also offer insight on best practices for GPS Cybersecurity and designing robust navigation systems.

    About the Presenter:

    Roi Mit

He is a sales and marketing expert, has a background in both corporate and startup companies.     Dror Katalan

He also led large scale testing projects with military and defense agencies around the globe. And as Product Manager for Applied Materials in the field of mask inspection. Dror holds a Bachelor’s Degree in Laser Optical and Mechanical Engineering and a Master’s Degree in Mechanical Engineering both from the Technion – Israel Institute of Technology.
